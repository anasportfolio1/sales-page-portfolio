Show Me How It Works

You don't have a discipline problem.
You have a decision problem.

Monday, you're locked in. Greek yogurt, a real lunch, chicken thawing for dinner. You've got this.

Then Wednesday happens. A meeting runs long. A deadline moves up. By 8:47pm you're standing in front of an open fridge with zero ideas and even less energy, and the food-delivery app is already open before you've decided anything.

So you tell yourself the same thing you told yourself last month: next week I'll be more disciplined. You'll meal-prep. You'll track everything. You'll finally be consistent.

And for about three days, you are. Then the week wins again.

It's exhausting. Not the eating. The endless deciding. Forty small food decisions a day, every one of them made on an empty tank at the worst possible moment.

Here's the part nobody tells you: the people who eat well week after week aren't more disciplined than you. They're not stronger. They just stopped relying on willpower. They removed the decision entirely.
